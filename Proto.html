General

IoT protocols are broadly classified into two major categories. These are:
IoT data protocols (Presentation / Application layers); and
Network protocols for IoT (Datalink / Physical layers)
Initially protocol developments were proprietary. Now there is a growing trend towards standardization in all areas of IoT protocol

Network Protocols

IoT network protocols are used to connect devices over a network. Each IoT protocol enables either device-to-device, device-to-gateway or device-to-cloud/data center communication - or combinations of those communications. While the TCP/IP based protocols are becoming growingly dominant, some non-IP protocols are widely used in IoT networks. Some of the well known IoT network protocols are WiFi, Bluetooth, LoRaWAN, ZigBee, Z-Wave, and LPWAN

Data Protocols

Various data protocols offere certain capability that make it prefereable over others for a specific IoT deployment. The most commonly used data protocols are: MQQTT, XML, CoAP, AMQP, DDS, and WebSocket

Standard Protocols

To minimize the problems of interoperability and the design and deployment of IoT, some standardization efforts has been initiated. By now some standards have been published and are being implemented as a result of these initiatives. In this respect MATTER is the latest and widely accepted by several prominent tech companies

Platforms

IoT platforms are solutions that simplify and facilitate IoT deployment and application. The use of IoT platforms eliminates the need for building IoT systems from scratch resulting in a speedy deployment and better performance in operation. There are several types of platforms such as Connectivity, Device Management, Application Enablemet, Business Rules, Niche (such as AI/ML), etc. platforms.
Platforms may be open-source or proprietary. The choice of a platform is a complicated task. While the main criteria for the choice of a platform is naturally performance there are a number of other critical criteria to consider
